Why Check Your Credit Score?

Regularly monitoring your credit score helps you:

  • Detect errors or fraudulent activity early
  • Understand your financial standing
  • Improve your chances for loan approval
  • Negotiate better interest rates

Report vs Score: What's the Difference?

Credit Report

  • Detailed history of accounts
  • Lists all credit inquiries
  • Shows payment history
  • Contains personal information

Credit Score

  • Three-digit number (300-850)
  • Calculated from report data
  • Multiple scoring models exist
  • Snapshot of creditworthiness

Free Ways to Check

  • AnnualCreditReport.com - Official site for free annual reports
  • Credit Karma - Free weekly scores and monitoring
  • Bank/Credit Card Offers - Many institutions provide free scores

Understanding Different Score Types

FICO Score

Used by 90% of lenders, with industry-specific versions:

  • FICO 8: Most common general version
  • FICO Auto Score: For vehicle loans
  • FICO Bankcard Score: For credit cards

VantageScore

Created by the three bureaus, gaining popularity:

  • Uses same 300-850 range
  • More forgiving of occasional late payments
  • Considers trended data

Paid Services

  • FICO Score - Direct from the source (most widely used)
  • Experian Boost - Potentially increase your score
  • Identity Protection - Many include credit monitoring

How to Read Your Credit Report

Personal Information

Verify name, address, SSN for accuracy

Account History

Check for late payments, closed accounts, balances

Inquiries

Hard inquiries stay for 2 years (affect score 1 year)

Public Records

Bankruptcies, tax liens, or judgments if applicable

Disputing Credit Report Errors

1

Identify errors on all three bureau reports

2

Gather supporting documentation

3

Submit dispute online (fastest method)

4

Bureaus have 30 days to investigate

5

Review results and follow up if needed

Special Credit Situations

Thin Files

If you have limited credit history, consider:

  • Becoming an authorized user
  • Getting a secured credit card
  • Using credit builder products

Business Owners

Separate business credit includes:

  • DUNS number from Dun & Bradstreet
  • Business credit scores (Paydex)
  • Commercial credit reports
