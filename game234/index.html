Why Your Credit Score Matters

A good credit score opens doors to better interest rates, higher loan amounts, and more financial opportunities.

Your three-digit number (typically 300-850) affects nearly every aspect of your financial life, from renting an apartment to getting a cell phone contract.

Credit Score Myths Debunked

Myth

Checking your score lowers it

Truth

Only hard inquiries from lenders affect your score. Checking your own score is a soft inquiry.

Myth

You need to carry a balance

Truth

Paying in full each month is best. Carrying a balance just costs you interest.
